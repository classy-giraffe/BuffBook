## 6.2 Designing a Mesocycle: The Four Levers

Regardless of the specific goal, you have the same four levers to shape the stimulus:

1. **Exercise selection** – cover the muscle’s full contractile range and joint actions.
2. **Sequencing** – place priority work first in the session and early in the week (see Section 5.6).
3. **Volume reallocation** – give slightly more hard sets to the muscles you want to emphasise, while taking from muscles that can temporarily receive less.
4. **Freshness** – train your priority when you are most recovered.

You do not need to ramp volume week after week. The double progression model (add reps, then add load) provides the increasing overload signal while the weekly volume stays constant. A deload is only inserted if performance genuinely decays—it is reactive, not scheduled.
