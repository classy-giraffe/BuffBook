## 6.4 Program Example: Upper/Lower with Glute, Side Delt, and Lat Emphasis

*> Context:* A female lifter, intermediate, training 4 days per week. She wants to emphasise glutes (overall shape), side delts (capped shoulders), and lats (back width). Her calves already receive significant fractional volume from leg curls and walking, so direct calf work is minimal. She uses the same Lower and Upper workouts twice a week.
{> : .practical }

**Split**
- Day 1 (Monday): Lower
- Day 2 (Tuesday): Upper
- Day 3 (Thursday): Lower (same session as Day 1)
- Day 4 (Friday): Upper (same session as Day 2)
- Wed, Sat, Sun: Rest

**Lower Workout (performed Monday and Thursday)**
- Exercise order follows the lengthened‑to‑shortened logic within each muscle group. Glute work is placed first.

| Order | Muscle | Exercise | Sets per session | Reps | RIR | Notes |
|-------|--------|----------|------------------|------|-----|-------|
| 1 | Glutes | Barbell hip thrust | 3 | 6–10 | 1–2 | Lengthened glute emphasis; load through full hip extension |
| 2 | Quads + Glutes | Heels‑elevated goblet squat (deep) | 3 | 6–10 | 1–2 | Additional glute and quad lengthened stimulus |
| 3 | Hamstrings | Seated leg curl | 3 | 6–10 | 1–2 | Distal hamstring emphasis, hip flexed |
| 4 | Quads | Leg extension | 2 | 6–10 | 1–2 | Shortened quad emphasis; rectus femoris trained at longer hip angle |
| 5 | Hamstrings (secondary) | 45‑degree back extension (glute‑focused) | 2 | 10–15 | 1–2 | Hip‑extension movement; add load as needed |
| 6 | Glutes (finisher) | Cable kickback | 2 | 12–15 | 1 | Peak contraction glute work |

**Upper Workout (performed Tuesday and Friday)**
- Emphasis on lateral delts and lats. Direct chest volume is reduced because pressing provides fractional triceps and anterior delt stimulation.

| Order | Muscle | Exercise | Sets per session | Reps | RIR | Notes |
|-------|--------|----------|------------------|------|-----|-------|
| 1 | Lats | Wide‑grip lat pulldown | 3 | 6–10 | 1–2 | Full stretch at top; use straps |
| 2 | Side delts | Cable lateral raise (behind the body) | 3 | 6–10 | 1–2 | Lengthened delt, constant tension |
| 3 | Chest | Slight‑decline dumbbell press | 2 | 6–10 | 1–2 | Chest still receives work; minimal shoulder strain |
| 4 | Upper back | Chest‑supported dumbbell row (elbows flared) | 2 | 6–10 | 1–2 | Scapular retraction + rear delt |
| 5 | Side delts (secondary) | Dumbbell lateral raise (seated, strict) | 2 | 12–15 | 1 | Shorter length, metabolic stress |
| 6 | Triceps | Overhead cable extension (rope) | 2 | 10–15 | 1 | Long head in stretch; fractional volume from pressing avoids need for high volume |
| 7 | Biceps (optional) | Dumbbell curl | 1 | 10–15 | 1 | Biceps receive fractional volume from pulldowns and rows |

**Weekly Volume by Muscle (Hard Sets)**
- Glutes: 4 (hip thrust) + 2 (back extension) + 2 (kickback) = 8 direct, plus indirect from squats ≈ 9–10
- Hamstrings: 6 direct (seated curl + back extension)
- Quadriceps: 6 direct (squat + leg extension)
- Lats: 6 direct (pulldown), indirect from rows
- Side delts: 6 direct (two exercises) plus some indirect from rows
- Chest: 4 direct (decline press)
- Triceps: 4 direct + pressing fractional
- Biceps: 2 direct + pulling fractional

**Progression**
- Double progression on all exercises. Reps 6–10, add load when top of range is reached with RIR 1–2.
- Expected mesocycle duration: 10–12 weeks, or until the lifter is satisfied with glute and delt improvement, then shift emphasis elsewhere.
