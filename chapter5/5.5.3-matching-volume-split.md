### 5.5.3: Matching Volume to the Split

The choice of split (Upper/Lower, Push/Pull/Legs, Full Body) matters less than how you distribute the hard sets. A Push/Pull/Legs done twice per week (PPLPPLR) with 6 hard sets per muscle per session produces the same weekly frequency (2x) as an Upper/Lower (ULULRRR) with 6 sets per muscle per session, just with different daily grouping. Choose the split that best accommodates your schedule, enjoyment, and recovery capacity.

- **Full Body (3–4x/week):** Great for beginners/intermediates with good work capacity, allows high frequency, lower per‑session fatigue. Harder to accumulate high volume on individual muscles without sessions running very long.
- **Upper/Lower (4x/week):** Excellent for intermediates, balances frequency and volume, easy to slot into a weekly routine.
- **Push/Pull/Legs (6x/week):** High frequency, high volume potential, but demanding on recovery and schedule.
- **Modified Bro Split (5x/week, each muscle 2x):** For advanced lifters who need to concentrate volume on specific weak points but still want some direct frequency on all muscles.
