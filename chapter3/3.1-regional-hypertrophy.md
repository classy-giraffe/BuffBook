## 3.1 Regional Hypertrophy: Why the Shape of the Load Matters

A muscle does not grow as a uniform block. The quadriceps, hamstrings, triceps, and pectorals all display regional differences in hypertrophy depending on how they are trained. Understanding why this happens is one of the most practically valuable skills in exercise selection. Two major theoretical frameworks have emerged to explain regional hypertrophy: **Stretch-Mediated Hypertrophy (SMH)** and **Neuromechanical Matching (NMM)**. While they are sometimes presented as opposing views, they may in fact describe complementary mechanisms that operate through different pathways and potentially produce different structural outcomes. This section presents both theories and examines where they agree, where they diverge, and what the current evidence actually supports.
