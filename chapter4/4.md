# Chapter 4: Exercise Selection & Equipment

Chapters 1 to 4 established *what* drives hypertrophy (high MUR + high fiber-level tension) and *where* on a muscle that stimulus lands (regional specificity, lengthened position bias). This chapter translates those principles into concrete criteria for selecting exercises and equipment. Every exercise in your program should be there for a reason. By the end of this chapter you will be able to evaluate any exercise—free weight, cable, or machine—against a set of non-negotiable principles, and to assemble a menu of movements that systematically covers a muscle’s full contractile range while avoiding the pitfalls that undermine tension.
