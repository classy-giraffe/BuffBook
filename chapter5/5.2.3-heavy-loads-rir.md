### 5.2.3: Why Heavy Loads Can Tolerate Higher RIR

Heavy loads (1–5 RM, >85% 1RM) demand full motor unit recruitment from the very first rep, because the external force requirement is high enough to force the nervous system to recruit its largest motor neurons immediately [13]. This means a set of 3 reps at RIR 2 (i.e., a load you could lift for 5 reps) still provides near‑maximal MU recruitment on every rep, and the slow bar speed inherent to heavy lifting ensures high fiber‑level tension. Light loads (15–30 RM) must be taken much closer to failure (RIR 0–1) to achieve the same MU recruitment, because the early reps are easy and only recruit low‑threshold MUs. This is not a mark against light loads; it simply means the RIR/RPE target must be adjusted downwards as load decreases.
