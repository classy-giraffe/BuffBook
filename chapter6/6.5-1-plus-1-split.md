## 6.5 When You Want Different Exercises for a Muscle: The 1+1 Split

If you decide a muscle needs two distinct exercises to cover its regions, and you are training it twice a week, you can either put both exercises on both days (2+2 total) or put one exercise on day 1 and the other on day 2 (1+1 each day).

The 1+1 split can be useful when:
- You want to emphasise a biarticular head that another exercise largely misses (e.g., rectus femoris if you squat heavy vs. leg extension).
- You want each day to train a different contractile range for variety or joint management.

The cost is slightly more setup and slightly less reliable per‑exercise progression tracking, because each exercise only gets one data point per session. For a dedicated weak‑point block where a particular head is lagging, the 1+1 approach is a logical way to give that head a true 2× frequency. For general hypertrophy programs, the repeated‑workout approach works just as well and is easier to run. Both are valid; neither is “wrong.”
